AI Session - Lesson 3

Prompt practice

Good prompts help AI give better answers. The more context, constraints, and goals you give it, the more useful the result usually becomes.

A prompt is like giving instructions to a teammate. If you are clear, specific, and honest about what you already know, the response is more likely to be useful.

Prompt Comparison: Bad vs Good

Bad Prompt Too vague

"Fix this."

Why it's bad: No context, no specifics, no indication of what you've tried or what the problem actually is.

Good Prompt Clear and specific

"My buttons are stacking vertically on mobile. Explain why that might happen and show me one CSS change to test first."

Why it's good: Describes the problem, asks for explanation, requests one specific solution to try.

Strong Prompt Formula

Template Use this structure

"I am building [thing]. I have already tried [x]. Please explain [problem] in simple terms and give me one next step."

This formula provides context, shows your effort, and requests a manageable next step.

Prompt Checklist

  • Say what you are building - Give context about your project
  • Say what you already tried - Show your thinking process
  • Ask for one small step at a time - Keep it manageable
  • Ask it to explain in simple language - Ensure you understand

Recommended Workflow

Process Three-step habit

  1. Ask AI for a draft - Get initial suggestions
  2. Check it line by line - Understand every part
  3. Improve it yourself - Add your own thinking and style

A strong workflow lets AI build the rough skeleton, then you keep checking, improving, and adding your own ideas.

Activity Compare and choose

  1. Build a tiny HTML section on your own first
  2. Ask AI to improve it
  3. Keep the version you understand best
  4. Explain why you chose it

Important: If AI gives you code you cannot read, ask it to slow down or rewrite it in simpler steps.

Pro Tips

Second-opinion trick

If the answer matters, ask a second AI or another person to sanity check it before you use it. Different checks catch different mistakes.

Interview-ready line

"I use AI for the heavy lifting, but I still review the code carefully, improve it myself, and verify the final result before I move on."
