AI Session

Using AI without losing your own voice

This session shows you when AI can help, when it should stay out of the way, and how to keep people in charge of the final result.

The goal is not to become dependent on AI. The goal is to become a developer who knows when to use it, when to question it, and how to explain every decision clearly in a team or job interview.

AI What AI helps with

AI is useful when you already have a goal and want help getting there faster.

  • Explaining code in plain English
  • Brainstorming ideas for a page or feature
  • Finding mistakes or suggesting improvements
  • Drafting a first version that you can reshape yourself

Human What people still do

Humans still own the work. That is what employers care about.

  • Choose the goal and define what good looks like
  • Decide if the answer is actually correct
  • Test the result and fix what breaks
  • Talk through trade-offs with teammates and clients

Career Why this matters in jobs

Interviewers want to know whether you can think, collaborate, and verify, not just copy output.

  • Say how you checked an AI answer
  • Explain when you ignored AI and why
  • Show that you can work independently first
  • Show that you can improve work with feedback

Your activities

This session includes a compare-and-improve workflow so you can feel the difference yourself.

Activity 1: Build without AI first

Build a small task without AI first so you can see your own process and understand the fundamentals.

Activity 2: Rebuild with AI assistance

Repeat the same task with AI and compare speed, clarity, and confidence. Notice what changed.

Activity 3: Revisit and improve

Revisit an earlier lesson with AI support and explain what changed in your own words.

Activity 4: Human review

Review the final answer with another person before calling it finished. Human feedback is essential.

The key message

AI is a tool for developers, not a replacement for them. The best results come from people choosing the goal, checking the work, and shaping the final product together.

When you are ready, start the session and move through the lessons in order.

Interview answer starter

If someone asks how you use AI, a strong answer sounds like this:

"I use AI to help me understand problems and speed up small tasks, but I always check the result, test the code, and make sure I can explain what it does."

What you should be able to do by the end

Write better prompts
Craft clear, specific requests that get useful AI responses
Spot AI mistakes
Identify when AI suggestions are incorrect or incomplete
Compare approaches
Evaluate manual solutions versus AI-assisted ones
Explain your process
Clearly communicate your decisions to others
